What’s included in a typical brand kit?

A tidy folder of assets you can plug straight into your website, social and print.

Logo files

Main logo, simplified mark and variations in light/dark and transparent PNG/SVG formats.

Colour palette

Primary, secondary and accent colours — with HEX/RGB codes so they can be used anywhere.

Typography choices

Heading and body fonts, usually from free web-safe sources, with usage suggestions.

Examples & mockups

Preview of how your brand looks on a homepage, social post or simple business card.

Usage guidelines

Short notes on spacing, colour combinations and “do’s & don’ts” to keep things consistent.

Files ready for your designer/printer

Organised folders you can hand to any designer, printer or VA without confusion.

How the branding process works

Friendly, collaborative and straightforward — even if you’re “not a design person”.

1. Short brand chat

We talk about your audience, vibe, goals and any references you like or dislike.

2. Mood & direction

We explore a few visual directions and agree on a style that feels right for you.

3. Logo & palette

We design a core logo, colour palette and typography that work together.

4. Refinements

You’ll have a chance to request tweaks so everything feels spot-on.

5. Brand kit delivery

You receive organised files and a simple guide in a shared folder or via your client portal, so everything is easy to find later.

Curious how the portal works? See the BrightFoundry client portal in detail →

6. Optional add-ons

We can create social templates, banners or simple print designs if you need them.
