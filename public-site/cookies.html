Cookies Policy

How BrightFoundry uses cookies.

This policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can control them.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your actions and preferences and are widely used to make websites work more efficiently.

2. How we use cookies

BrightFoundry uses cookies to:

  • Enable basic website functionality
  • Improve performance and user experience
  • Understand how visitors use the site, where analytics are enabled
  • Protect security and prevent abuse

3. Types of cookies we use

3.1 Essential cookies

These cookies are required for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They include things like navigation, session management and security.

3.2 Analytics cookies

If enabled, we may use analytics tools, such as Google Analytics or Plausible, to collect anonymous information about how visitors use the site.

Data collected may include:

  • Pages viewed
  • Time spent on the site
  • Links clicked
  • Device and browser information

3.3 Third-party cookies

Some pages may contain embedded content from third-party services, such as video, forms or scheduling tools. These services may set their own cookies under their own policies.

4. Cookie consent

On your first visit, you may see a cookie notice asking you to accept or manage cookies. You can choose to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Change your preferences at any time

Essential cookies do not require consent.

5. How to disable cookies

You can manage or disable cookies at any time through your browser settings. Common browser settings usually include:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies & Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies & Site Permissions

Turning off cookies may affect how the website functions.

6. Updates to this policy

We may update this Cookies Policy periodically to reflect changes in technology or regulation. Updates will be posted on this page.
